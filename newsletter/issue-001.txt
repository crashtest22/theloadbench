📦 COMPONENT WATCH

H4350 — Check Powder Valley and Graf's first. Moves fast when in stock. Check Friday mornings — that's when restocks typically hit.

Varget — Widener's has been the most consistent lately. .308 guys, you know the drill.

N140 / N150 — Harder to find in quantity. Graf's usually has the best Vihtavuori stock. Grab when you see it — these don't sit long.

Federal 210M / 205M — Still tight. If you find Federal match primers anywhere near MSRP, buy a sleeve. CCI BR-4 is a solid alternative for small primers if you're bridging a gap.

6GT Brass — Alpha was spotted in stock on r/6gt this week. Peterson and Lapua are the other two worth watching. Midsouth and Graf's are your best online bets.

🎯 LOAD DATA SPOTLIGHT: 6GT

The 6GT is earning its place in PRS and F-class — and for good reason. It outperforms 6mm Creedmoor in efficiency without beating up your brass.

105gr Berger Hybrid / 108gr Berger BT:
• Powder: H4350 or N140, 30–32gr range
• Primer: Federal 205M or CCI BR-4
• Brass: Alpha or Peterson (more consistent neck tension than Lapua in this cartridge)
• Velocity target: 2,950–3,050 fps depending on barrel length
• SD goal: Under 8 fps is achievable. Over 12 fps? Look at primer seating depth and brass prep before chasing powder charge.

115gr DTAC / Berger Hybrid:
• Powder: N150 or H4831sc, 30–31gr range
• Seating depth: Start 0.020" off the lands and work from there

Always work up from minimum. These are reference points from real bench experience — not a prescription.

🔧 FROM THE BENCH

Brass prep matters more than powder charge with the 6GT. Full-length size, uniform primer pockets, consistent neck tension — that's where your SD gains actually live. Powder charge is the last variable you should be chasing.
